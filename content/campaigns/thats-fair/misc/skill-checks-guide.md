---
title: Skill Checks Guide
type: misc
---

| Skill | Usage Rank | Role Playing | Combat |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Perception | 1 | Spot hidden enemies, lurking ambushes, or concealed observers before they strike<br><br>Notice small details others miss: bloodstains, drag marks, recent disturbances in dust or foliage<br><br>Hear distant sounds like approaching guards, rushing water, or collapsing structures as early warnings | Win initiative by noticing hostile intent before combat officially starts<br><br>Spot hidden or invisible enemies during their turn to alert allies and negate advantage<br><br>Notice environmental changes mid-combat like reinforcements arriving or traps being activated |
| Investigation | 2 | Search crime scenes for hidden clues, secret compartments, or overlooked evidence<br><br>Deduce trap mechanisms, puzzle solutions, or the purpose of mysterious machinery through logical analysis<br><br>Find concealed doors, hidden passages, or structural weaknesses in walls and floors | Identify environmental hazards to exploit (chandeliers to drop, weak floors to collapse, explosive barrels)<br><br>Analyze enemy formations to spot tactical weaknesses or identify who's the leader/support<br><br>Locate hidden enemies, invisible foes, or camouflaged creatures through careful observation |
| Stealth | 3 | Scout ahead to identify enemy positions, trap locations, and patrol patterns before the party commits<br><br>Tail suspects through crowded streets or wilderness without being detected<br><br>Hide evidence, conceal your presence at crime scenes, or eavesdrop on private conversations | Hide between turns to gain advantage on attacks and force enemies to waste actions searching<br><br>Sneak into flanking positions to gain advantage and help set up ambushes<br><br>Reposition without drawing attention to reach vulnerable targets like archers or spellcasters |
| Insight | 4 | Detect lies, half-truths, or omissions during negotiations, interrogations, or social encounters<br><br>Read body language and emotional states to identify who's trustworthy versus who's planning betrayal<br><br>Sense when NPCs are charmed, possessed, or replaced by noticing behavioral inconsistencies | Predict enemy tactics by reading their body language and fighting stance<br><br>Find out what weaknesses or vulnerabilities an enemy possesses<br><br>Identify which foes are likely to flee or surrender based on their morale and demeanor |
| Persuasion | 5 | Negotiate favorable deals, lower prices, or gain concessions from reasonable NPCs<br><br>Convince NPCs to help the party, share information, or provide resources voluntarily<br><br>Recruit allies, build alliances, or turn neutral parties into supporters of your cause | Convince intelligent enemies to stand down, switch sides, or reveal information mid-combat<br><br>Rally frightened NPCs or wavering allies to continue fighting instead of fleeing<br><br>Negotiate temporary truces or ceasefires when combat becomes disadvantageous |
| Athletics | 6 | Break down doors, bust open locked chests, or force open barred gates when stealth isn't an option<br><br>Intimidate through physical displays like bending iron bars, crushing objects, or arm-wrestling contests<br><br>Climb sheer surfaces, scale walls during escapes, or traverse difficult vertical terrain without equipment | Shove enemies off ledges, into hazards, or away from vulnerable allies<br><br>Grapple opponents to lock down dangerous spellcasters or prevent enemies from fleeing<br><br>Break free from restraints, grapples, or being swallowed by creatures |
| Acrobatics | 7 | Navigate narrow ledges, tightrope walk across chasms, or balance on precarious surfaces during exploration<br><br>Perform impressive physical feats to entertain crowds, win contests, or gain entry to performer's guilds<br><br>Squeeze through tight spaces like ventilation shafts, collapsed passages, or prison bars | Escape grapples by twisting free from an enemy's grip<br><br>Maintain footing on slippery, icy, or unstable terrain during battle<br><br>Tumble through enemy spaces to reach priority targets or avoid opportunity attacks |
| Deception | 8 | Lie convincingly to guards, officials, or enemies to talk your way out of trouble<br><br>Create believable disguises or cover stories to infiltrate enemy organizations<br><br>Bluff during negotiations to make your position seem stronger than it actually is | Feint or create distractions mid-combat to mislead enemies about your true target or intentions<br><br>Impersonate allies or enemy commanders to cause confusion in enemy ranks<br><br>Bluff about reinforcements, hidden traps, or the party's remaining resources to cause enemies to hesitate |
| Arcana | 9 | Identify magical items, decipher arcane symbols, or understand the purpose of ritual circles<br><br>Recall knowledge about magical creatures, planar entities, and their weaknesses or behaviors<br><br>Detect and analyze magical traps, wards, or illusions before the party triggers them | Recognize enemy spells being cast to warn allies what's coming (e.g., "That's Fireball—spread out!")<br><br>Identify the magical nature of enemy abilities to determine damage types and resistances<br><br>Determine which magical effect is sustaining an enemy (concentration spell, glyph, artifact) to target the source |
| Intimidation | 10 | Extract information through threats, forcing NPCs to reveal secrets they'd otherwise protect<br><br>Establish dominance in social situations to gain respect from criminals, mercenaries, or warriors<br><br>Discourage potential threats from attacking by making clear the consequences of hostility | Force weaker enemies to flee or surrender without further bloodshed<br><br>Create openings by making enemies hesitate, flinch, or fight defensively<br><br>Break enemy morale after killing their leader or strongest member with a terrifying display |
| Sleight of Hand | 11 | Pickpocket keys, documents, or coin purses from guards and NPCs without detection<br><br>Plant evidence, stolen goods, or tracking devices on unsuspecting targets<br><br>Conceal weapons, tools, or contraband during inspections or pat-downs | Disarm traps mid-combat without taking your full action<br><br>Steal spell components, holy symbols, or material focuses from enemy casters<br><br>Quick-draw hidden backup weapons when disarmed or make feints to create openings |
| History | 12 | Recall ancient prophecies, historical events, or past wars that provide context for current quests<br><br>Identify valuable historical artifacts, artwork, or relics that others might overlook<br><br>Recognize heraldry, noble lineages, or organizational symbols to navigate political situations | Remember tactical information about famous battles fought in the current location to exploit terrain<br><br>Recall historical weaknesses or defeats of legendary enemies you're currently facing<br><br>Identify ancient war constructs, undead generals, or historical warriors and know their signature tactics |
| Survival | 13 | Track creatures through wilderness by following footprints, broken branches, or other signs<br><br>Forage for food and water, find shelter, and navigate without getting lost in hostile terrain<br><br>Predict natural dangers like avalanches, flash floods, or animal migration patterns | Identify the most defensible terrain positions for your party before enemies arrive<br><br>Track fleeing enemies to prevent escape or follow them to their base<br><br>Navigate difficult terrain efficiently during combat (forests, swamps, mountains) while enemies struggle |
| Nature | 14 | Identify safe plants for food/medicine versus poisonous varieties during wilderness survival<br><br>Predict weather patterns to prepare for storms, find shelter, or plan optimal travel times<br><br>Track animals or recognize territorial signs to avoid dangerous beasts or find prey | Recall beast and plant creature vulnerabilities, resistances, and special abilities<br><br>Exploit natural terrain features like quicksand, thorny growth, or unstable ground against enemies<br><br>Predict animal behavior to calm, provoke, or redirect wild creatures during encounters |
| Performance | 15 | Entertain crowds to gather information, earn coin, or create diversions for party members<br><br>Impersonate specific individuals convincingly using acting skills rather than just disguise<br><br>Win contests, gain entry to performance venues, or earn favor with artistic patrons | Distract enemies with flashy displays, drawing attention away from more vulnerable allies<br><br>Demoralize foes through taunting performances that highlight their failures or fears<br><br>Inspire allies mid-battle through war chants, battle cries, or heroic displays (outside Bardic Inspiration) |
| Animal Handling | 16 | Calm aggressive animals to pass through their territory safely or gain them as temporary allies<br><br>Train mounts for combat, teach animals tricks, or establish long-term bonds with animal companions<br><br>Read animal body language to determine if they're diseased, cursed, or under magical control | Control your mount during combat to execute complex maneuvers or prevent it from bolting<br><br>Calm an enemy's mount or beast companion, potentially dismounting riders or removing combatants<br><br>Redirect aggressive animals toward enemies or away from vulnerable party members |
| Medicine | 17 | Stabilize dying NPCs or party members without using spell slots or healing potions<br><br>Diagnose diseases, poisons, or curses to determine proper treatment or necessary spells<br><br>Perform field surgery, set broken bones, or treat wounds during short rests for faster recovery | Stabilize fallen allies at 0 HP without using actions for healing spells or potions<br><br>Identify poison or disease effects on allies mid-combat to inform treatment priorities<br><br>Determine if "dead" enemies are truly deceased or merely unconscious/playing dead |
| Religion | 18 | Identify holy symbols, divine artifacts, or religious texts to determine deity affiliations<br><br>Recall religious customs, holy days, or proper temple etiquette to gain favor with faithful NPCs<br><br>Understand undead creation methods, celestial hierarchies, or fiendish pacts | Recognize undead types and their specific vulnerabilities (radiant damage, turning immunity, etc.)<br><br>Identify divine spells being cast by enemy clerics or paladins<br><br>Recall celestial or fiendish weaknesses, resistances, and behavioral patterns during extraplanar encounters |
